Frequently Asked Questions

A sump pump is a device used to remove accumulated water from a sump basin, typically found in the basement of homes. It's essential to prevent flooding and water damage, especially in areas prone to heavy rainfall or with high water tables.

You may need a sump pump if your basement has experienced flooding in the past, if you notice dampness or water stains, or if you live in an area with a high groundwater level. Consulting with a professional can help determine the necessity.

While DIY installation is possible for those with plumbing skills, it's recommended to hire a professional for proper placement and to ensure all codes and regulations are met, guaranteeing long-term effectiveness.

The main types of sump pumps include submersible pumps, which are installed within the sump pit, and pedestal pumps, which have the motor above the basin. Submersible pumps are generally quieter and more efficient for use in finished basements.

It's advisable to test your sump pump at least once every three months and conduct any maintenance as recommended by the manufacturer. Regular checks ensure the pump functions correctly when needed, especially before rainy seasons.
